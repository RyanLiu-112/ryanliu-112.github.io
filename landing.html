Postdoctoral Associate at the University of Florida
RNA modification biology, stress response, autophagy, and metabolic disease.

About

I am a postdoctoral associate in the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Biology at the UF Health Cancer Center, University of Florida. My academic training spans biological engineering, biochemistry and molecular biology, biological medicine, cell biology, and RNA biology. I am interested in how RNA modifications and stress-response pathways shape cellular homeostasis and disease-related metabolism.

Academic Experience

  • RNA m6A modifications during ER stress, University of Florida, Postdoc Researcher and Project Leader, 2025 - Present. Using GLORI 2.0 to assess the cellular m6A landscape in response to ER stress.
  • Surface-associated RNA and glycosylation, Yale University, Postdoc Researcher and Project Leader, 2024 - Present. Investigated cell surface-associated RNA and glycoRNA dynamics during adipogenesis using bioorthogonal labeling, rPAL, and APEX2 proximity labeling.
  • Influence of ALKBH1 on cellular autophagy, Jinan University, Ph.D. Candidate and Project Leader, 2018 - 2024. Studied how ALKBH1 influences autophagy and glucose metabolism through lysosomal dysfunction and chaperone-mediated autophagy.
  • ALKBH1 regulates IRE1alpha m6A methylation, Jinan University, Ph.D. Candidate and Project Leader, 2018 - 2024. Explored ALKBH1-mediated m6A demethylation of IRE1alpha in liver lipotoxicity and endoplasmic reticulum stress response.
